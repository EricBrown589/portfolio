How I Got Into Coding

I got into computers and technology at a young age. My first introduction was through computer gaming, which started with games such as: Command and Conquer: Generals, Sid Meier's Civilization III, and Black & White. I started finding myself thinking about how these games worked, as I started getting older I would find myself wondering more and more how computers and the internet worked altogether. I would continue to question those things and wonder about them for years to come. I eventually got my first taste at coding when I was 16 years old and my dad had got me a Raspberry Pi. I didn't know how to use it or what to use it for but it introduced me into the world of coding. A few years later, when I was around the age of 19, I tried to teach myself how to make a game, I used Google and Youtube as my teachers. That however, did not go so well, there was a lot I still didn't understand and still needed to learn which was difficult to learn without an actual teacher. Queue the present, I'm now in a software engineering class which was put together by the kind and wonderful people at Interapt and General Assembly. They came together to host this class for free and I was lucky enough to be accepted and offered a spot!
